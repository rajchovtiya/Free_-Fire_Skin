How Home Loan Interest Rates Are Calculated

Home loan interest rates are typically calculated using the monthly reducing balance method. Under this method, the interest is computed on the outstanding principal amount after each installment repayment. In the initial years, the EMI largely consists of the interest portion, which decreases as the principal is paid down over time.

How to Calculate Home Loan EMI?

To calculate your home loan EMI manually, use the following formula:

EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N-1]
Where:

  • P: Principal loan amount
  • R: Monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12)
  • N: Loan tenure in months

However, this formula can be complex and time-consuming to calculate manually. To simplify the process, you can use an online home loan EMI calculator to instantly calculate your EMI based on your loan amount, interest rate, and tenure.
