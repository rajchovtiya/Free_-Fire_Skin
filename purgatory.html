Money-Back Policies

Money-Back Policies are a type of life insurance policy that not only provide life coverage but also offer periodic returns (money-back) during the policy term. Unlike traditional life insurance plans that pay a lump sum amount on maturity or death, money-back policies provide a percentage of the sum assured at regular intervals. This makes them an attractive option for individuals seeking both life cover and a steady income stream.

What are Money-Back Policies?

Money-back policies are life insurance policies where the policyholder receives a portion of the sum assured at periodic intervals (usually every 3 or 5 years). The remaining amount, along with bonuses (if applicable), is paid out at the time of policy maturity or in case of the policyholder’s death. The periodic payouts make these policies ideal for people who want financial security along with regular cash flows during the term of the policy.

Key Features of Money-Back Policies

  • Periodic Payments: Money-back policies offer periodic payouts (usually every 3 to 5 years) of a percentage of the sum assured. This provides a source of regular income.
  • Life Coverage: These policies provide life insurance coverage, ensuring that your loved ones receive the full sum assured in case of your death during the policy term.
  • Maturity Benefit: At the end of the policy term, the policyholder receives the remaining sum assured along with any bonuses or additional benefits, provided the policyholder survives the term.
  • Bonus Facility: Many money-back policies participate in the insurer’s profit-sharing, which may result in bonuses being added to the policy value, thus enhancing the final payout.
  • Tax Benefits: Premiums paid for money-back policies are eligible for tax deductions under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, and the maturity benefit is generally tax-free under Section 10(10D).

Types of Money-Back Policies

  • Traditional Money-Back Policies: These policies provide guaranteed periodic payments, with the sum assured paid on maturity or death, and bonuses (if applicable) added to the total payout.
  • Unit Linked Money-Back Policies (ULMBs): These are market-linked policies where a portion of the premium is invested in equity or debt instruments. The returns on these policies depend on the performance of the underlying investments.
  • Child Money-Back Plans: These policies are designed to provide financial security for a child’s future. The policyholder receives money-back benefits during the policy term, and the child is provided with the sum assured at maturity, ensuring financial protection for their education or marriage.

Benefits of Money-Back Policies

  • Regular Income: The periodic payouts provide a steady stream of income, which can help meet your financial needs during the policy term (e.g., for children’s education, family expenses).
  • Life Insurance Protection: In case of the policyholder’s unfortunate death, the nominee receives the sum assured, along with any bonuses or additional benefits.
  • Financial Planning: Money-back policies can help in planning long-term financial goals, such as child’s education, marriage, or retirement, as they provide both life cover and a source of income.
  • Tax Benefits: The premiums paid qualify for tax deductions under Section 80C, and the maturity benefit (sum assured and bonuses) is generally exempt from tax under Section 10(10D).
  • Loan Facility: Many insurers allow policyholders to take loans against their money-back policies in case of emergencies, based on the policy’s surrender value.

Charges in Money-Back Policies

  • Premium Allocation Charges: These charges are deducted from the premium amount before the money is allocated towards life cover or investment. They cover the cost of acquiring and maintaining the policy.
  • Mortality Charges: These are charges for life coverage, which are deducted periodically from the policy’s value to cover the life risk of the policyholder.
  • Policy Administration Charges: These charges cover the administrative costs of the policy, including servicing and maintaining the policy records.
  • Fund Management Charges (for ULMBs): These charges are applicable for Unit Linked Money-Back Policies (ULMBs) where the premium is invested in market-linked instruments, such as equity or debt funds.

Eligibility Criteria for Money-Back Policies

  • Age: Money-back policies are typically available for individuals between the ages of 18 to 60 years. The minimum and maximum age may vary depending on the insurer and policy type.
  • Income: Policyholders should have a stable income source to ensure timely premium payments. Income proofs may be required for some policies.
  • Medical Underwriting: Some insurers may require a medical examination before approving the policy, especially for higher sum assured amounts or policyholders above a certain age.

Tax Benefits on Money-Back Policies

  • Section 80C Deductions: Premiums paid on money-back policies qualify for tax deductions under Section 80C, subject to a limit of ₹1.5 lakh per year.
  • Tax-Free Maturity Benefit: The benefits received at maturity (sum assured and bonuses) are tax-free under Section 10(10D), provided certain conditions are met.

How to Choose the Right Money-Back Policy

  • Define Your Financial Needs: Choose a policy that aligns with your financial goals, such as funding children’s education, retirement, or fulfilling specific personal needs.
  • Understand the Policy’s Returns: Compare the returns offered by various money-back policies, including bonuses (for traditional policies) and market-linked returns (for ULMBs).
  • Check the Charges: Ensure that the charges, such as premium allocation and mortality charges, are reasonable and do not significantly impact the returns on the policy.
  • Flexibility: Look for a policy that offers flexible premium payment options and allows you to adjust the sum assured or cover as your financial situation changes.

Conclusion

Money-back policies are ideal for individuals who want both life insurance protection and periodic cash returns during the term of the policy. These plans offer a combination of financial security and liquidity, making them suitable for individuals looking for a steady income stream while ensuring the protection of their family in case of unforeseen events. With tax benefits, flexible payment options, and guaranteed payouts, money-back policies are a great way to achieve long-term financial goals while safeguarding your loved ones.
